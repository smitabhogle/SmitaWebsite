Puppy Prosthetics: A Method for Mobility Rehabilitation Implementing 3D Design

Under the guidance of Dr. Kevin Crowthers, I completed an independent research project. My project was an engineering project where I designed and 3D printed a limb for my client, Socrates. Socrates is a dog who has an amputated left frontal limb.

Abstract

Graphical Abstract

Problem Statement

Many amputated dogs have limited mobility, and prosthetic limbs are expensive and not common for dogs. 

Engineering Statement

The objective of this project is to improve the mobility of a dog that has an amputated front left limb. The cost of this prosthetic will be lower compared to currently available prosthetics since it will be manufacturing them out of a more cost-effective and accessible material. The prosthetic must be functional, lightweight, and have the ability to withstand daily activities.

Analysis

The average speed of Socrates’ increased by 12.73% His angular movement decreased by 5.26%, proving increased stability and effectivity of the prosthetic limb.

Discussion

Analysis of the Decision Matrix leads to positive conclusions about the prosthetic leg. This device printed quickly, in approximately 10 hours. All the materials cost approximately $15. This leg not only helped my client regain mobility, but it allowed him to be able to wear a harness again. The ability to wear a harness enables Socrates to wear a leash and go on walks with his family.
