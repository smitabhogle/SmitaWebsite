Designing a Mechanized Dog Feeder for Wheelchair Users

Our client, Client X, is a wheelchair user who expressed their struggles in filling their dog's food bowl. We determined that the problem statement of our project is that the standard process of providing food to dogs suing a dog bowl on a floor with the products on the market is ill-adapted to individuals without full use of their legs. We met with Client X via zoom to in order to determine the criteria of our project. Our project objective was to construct a machine for dog feeding adapted for individuals using a wheelchair through mechanized dispensation of dog food. Attached to the right is our final project presentation.

Final Product

My group's preliminary design approaches included drawing many sketches for potential prototypes. Then, using cardboard, we made proof of concepts in order to determine the feasibility of our designs. Once our design was more solidified, I created the CAD for our prototype. After some time passed, we came to the conclusion that our initial designs were using up a lot of unneeded space and was over engineering a solution to the problem. This lead to many modifications to our design. Our final product is pictured on the left. 3D printed components, PVC pipes, and wood are the main materials used to build our project.
